OpenEndedGroup Field2 / AR

For this project, I partnered with an MFA graduate student to translate their physical sculptures into an interactive Augmented Reality experience. Their work frequently features hinged frames of differing dimensions and explores how their movement can reveal new interactions between the sculpture and the space it occupies. Our AR piece extends this notion with the common idea in AR of "a world inside a world".

I used Open-Ended Group's Field to place virtual objects in the viewer's live camera feed of the real-world environment, allowing the viewer to physically walk around the virtual sculpture. Our piece utilizes the OpenGL Stencil Buffer to only draw the light gray sculptures when they are viewed through a black frame. Each frame is paired with a single gray object, but overlapping frames will reveal all of their respective objects. By utilizing the Stencil Buffer in this way, we create a metaphysical world within the frames in this AR space.

OpenEndedGroup Field2 / AR

I was initially inspired by vines growing on the buildings on UChicago's campus. I decided to create a brush that allows you to draw these wandering tendrils in space. The root is drawn at a set distance from the camera while the offshoots randomly spiral away at an angle to the main branch.
