OpenEndedGroup Field2 / AR

These sketches were completed as assignments for my Augmented Reality Production course at UChicago. I created them using Field2, an AR program developed by my professor.

For the final project, I partnered with Julia Koreman, an MFA student at UChicago, to translate their digital sculptures into AR. Julia's work centers on hinged frames of differing dimensions and explores how their movement can reveal new interactions between the sculpture and the space it occupies.

Our AR piece plays with the common idea in AR of "worlds inside of worlds". Our piece utilizes the Stencil Buffer to only draw the light gray sculptures when they are viewed through a black frame. Each frame is paired with a single gray object, but overlapping frames will reveal all of their respective objects.

OpenEndedGroup Field2 / AR

The prompt for this assignment was "Lines". I was initially inspired by vines growing on the buildings on UChicago's campus. I decided to create a brush that allows you to draw these wandering tendrils in space. The root is drawn at a set distance from the camera while the offshoots randomly spiral away at an angle to the main branch.
sketch2
This sketch focused on exporting FBX / glTF files from Blender3D into Field2. I used photogrammetry program Meshroom to scan myself in my living room. I isolated the model and imported it into Field2. During the process, I ran into bandwidth issues when sending the file to my phone, so I had to drastically reduce the triangle count of the model.

I also made a very rough (and terrible looking) Rock, Paper, Scissors animation to test whether Field2 could run an imbedded animation.
