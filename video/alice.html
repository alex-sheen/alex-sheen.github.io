alice-01

Blender, AliceVision Meshroom
2021

I often find myself going down YouTube rabbit holes—especially about digital art—and that’s how I first discovered photogrammetry. alice-01 was the result. I began by 3D scanning my head and shoulders, then I duplicated the resulting mesh and applied a combination of modifiers to generate the web-like projections.

As for the name, I had just watched Alice in Wonderland and was experimenting with AliceVision Meshroom for the first time—thus the “alice-{n}” series was born.
alice-02

Blender, AliceVision Meshroom
2021

alice-02 was inspired by Midge Sinnaeve, a digital artist whose YouTube series explores the techniques behind his procedural and generative works.

For this piece, I photoscanned vines growing on the Kent Chemical Laboratory building at the University of Chicago. I then took a 3D base model of a human bust and unwrapped its mesh to a 2D plane so that I could use a Surface Deform modifier to project the scanned vine geometry onto the unwrapped mesh, allowing the organic texture to conform to the contours of the original form.
