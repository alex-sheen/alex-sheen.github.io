alice-01

Blender
AliceVision Meshroom
Date - 2021

I first heard about photogrammetry around this time, and alice-01 was the byproduct. I began with a photoscan of my own head and shoulder -- it's missing the other shoulder because I scanned myself in selfie-mode, but retroactively I'll call it an intentional creative decision :). I then duplicated the resulting mesh and used a combination of modifiers to create the web and grid-like projections.

As for the name, I had just watched Alice in Wonderland and was trying AliceVision Meshroom for the first time... hence the "alice-{n}" series!
alice-02

Blender
AliceVision Meshroom
Date - 2021

alice-02 was inspired by Midge Sinnaeve, a digital artist I found on YouTube. He has a video series where he walks through the process behind some of his work. For alice-02, I photoscanned vines growing on the Kent Chemical Laboratory building located on the University of Chicago's campus. I then took a 3D base model of a human bust and unwrapped its mesh to a 2D plane. Lastly, I projected the photoscanned vines onto the unwrapped mesh by applying a Surface Deform modifier to have the vine texture conform to the 2D unwrapped mesh.
