3D Diver Interface

Unity, Meta Oculus
2022

For CMSC 20300, Intro to Human-Computer Interaction, we were tasked with creating an intuitive and functional HUD interface for scuba divers.

Our design philosophy focused on providing fast access to frequently used functionality and inputs tailored for VR interactions.

To test our interface without a real scuba suit, we simulated the diver's underwater experience in virtual reality using an Oculus headset and a diving simulation running in Unity.

The left wrist provides quick access to high priority buttons for the Crewmates Manager and the Radio.

An SOS button is always accessible above for emergencies.
The Radio allows users to call crewmates.

Our horizontal numberpad design encourages a swipe-through motion which suits the VR environment better than a typical 2D button-tap interface. This design safeguards against user error and device inaccuracies with detecting hand and digit positions and depth from the headset.
The Crewmates Manager provides crewmate call and crewmate tracking buttons.

Our vertical interface design allows users to swipe horizontally through their desired button.
The right wrist provides real-time updates on Altitude and Time Spent Underwater.
A left hand pinch gesture triggers a green tracking arrow that points to the designated crewmate.
