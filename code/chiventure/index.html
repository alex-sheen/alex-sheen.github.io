This past spring I took a course on Software Development where I gained valuable experience in breaking down large software projects into manageable tasks. The class-wide project continued work on a custom game engine that was started the previous year, with my team focusing on a new battle system that would allow the player to fight enemies.

Along with practicing workflow techniques such as issue tracking, code reviews, and continuous integration, I also learned team management skills and how collaboration boosted our team’s productivity. From my past experience in game programming, I could think about games at a higher level, and I found that I gravitated toward a role of overseeing how all of our team’s pieces would come together and fit within the existing code architecture. Coordinating meetings with other teams and ensuring that our team had a clear design plan at all times prepared me for work on future projects.

I contributed around 2,000 lines of code. These files can be located primarily within the src/battle and tests/battle folders. I also created several Github Issues and Pull Requests
