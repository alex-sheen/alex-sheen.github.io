Assignment 4 | Python, HashCat, Jupiter Notebook
Password Cracking

Part 1: Fast Hashes
For Part 1, I used HashCat to crack 51% of a list of 400 password hashes -- SHA1($pass.$salt).
I used the following methods:

1. brute-force guesses, such as 5 digit numbers, 6 digit numbers, capitalized 5 letter word + two numbers at the end (e.g. Alex87, for an Alex born in 1987).
2. selected wordlists, such as passwords from the RockYou data breach.
3. wordslists + mangling, such as RockYou data breach + leetspeak rule set.

51% of 400 hashes took roughly 4 hours of computing time on Jupiter Notebook.
